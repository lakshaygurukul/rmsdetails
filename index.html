Rashtriya Military School (RMS) Criteria and Eligibility

Eligibility Criteria

1. Nationality

  • Only Indian citizens are eligible to apply.

2. Age Criteria

  • For Class VI Admission:
    • The candidate should be between 10 to 12 years of age as of March 31st of the year of admission.
    • The candidate should have been born between April 1, 2012, and March 31, 2014, for admission in 2024.
  • For Class IX Admission:
    • The candidate should be between 13 to 15 years of age as of March 31st of the year of admission.
    • The candidate should have been born between April 1, 2009, and March 31, 2011, for admission in 2024.
    • The candidate must have passed Class VIII from a recognized school at the time of admission.

3. Gender

  • Only boys are eligible for admission to Rashtriya Military Schools.

4. Reservation Policy

  • Scheduled Castes (SC): 15% of the seats are reserved.
  • Scheduled Tribes (ST): 7.5% of the seats are reserved.
  • Children of Service Personnel including Ex-Servicemen: 70% of the seats are reserved for children of JCOs/ORs (Junior Commissioned Officers/Other Ranks) in the Armed Forces.
  • Kashmir Migrants: A small number of seats are reserved for the wards of Kashmiri migrants.

5. Medical Fitness

  • Candidates must be medically fit as per the standards set by the Rashtriya Military Schools.

Rashtriya Military School Entrance Exam Pattern and Paper Marking

For Class VI Admission

  • Total Marks: 200
  • Subjects and Marking Scheme:
    • Mathematics:
      • Number of Questions: 40
      • Marks per Question: 2.5
      • Total Marks: 100
    • English:
      • Number of Questions: 40
      • Marks per Question: 2
      • Total Marks: 50
    • General Knowledge:
      • Number of Questions: 50
      • Marks per Question: 1
      • Total Marks: 50

For Class IX Admission

  • Total Marks: 300
  • Subjects and Marking Scheme:
    • Mathematics:
      • Number of Questions: 50
      • Marks per Question: 4
      • Total Marks: 200
    • English:
      • Number of Questions: 50
      • Marks per Question: 2
      • Total Marks: 100
    • General Science:
      • Number of Questions: 50
      • Marks per Question: 2
      • Total Marks: 100
    • Social Science:
      • Number of Questions: 50
      • Marks per Question: 2
      • Total Marks: 100

Paper Marking Summary

Class Total Marks
Class VI 200
Class IX 300

Selection Process

  • Interview: Candidates who qualify in the written exam are called for an interview. The interview carries 50 marks for both classes.
  • Final Merit List: The final merit list is prepared based on the performance in the written exam and interview, subject to medical fitness and other criteria.
  • The exam is conducted in English and Hindi, except for the English paper which is in English only.
